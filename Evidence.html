This article seeks to discover strategies to incorporate COVID-19 discussion into a social equity conversation about addressing health disparities. COVID19 provides various possibilities for policymakers to explore issues of social fairness in the context of public health. In particular, by focusing on health inequalities in regard to the disproportionate impact of COVID19 on minority and historically underprivileged groups, we may generate a much needed conversation on health outcomes for many communities. This article further investigates the application of COVID19's impact on disadvantaged individuals and communities of color for a discussion of options for reducing health inequalities. Economic position, race, and educational achievement are frequently associated with health inequalities and inequities. Social disparities are based on race/ethnicity, access to educational opportunities, and financial level, which can contribute to a lack of understanding about health care access, and poor health outcomes. Research of COVID-19, with a focus on the disproportionate impact on communities of color, can assist shape conversation and long-term action on the problem of larger health inequalities and solutions for addressing them.
